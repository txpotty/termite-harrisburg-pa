Detailed Termite Inspection

The first and most crucial step in effective termite control is a thorough and accurate inspection. Our experienced technicians are trained to identify the subtle signs of a termite infestation that many homeowners might miss. We conduct a comprehensive examination of your property, both interior and exterior, looking for evidence of termite activity. This includes searching for:

Signs of Termite Infestation

  • Mud Tubes: These are small mud tunnels built by subterranean termites to travel between their underground colony and their food source (your home's wood structure).

  • Damaged Wood: Termites eat wood from the inside out, leaving behind hollowed-out sections. This damage can appear as blistering, peeling, or discolored paint, or wood that sounds hollow when tapped.

  • Swarmers: These are winged reproductive termites that emerge from the colony to establish new ones, often seen in the spring.

  • Discarded Wings: Swarmers shed their wings after finding a mate, and you may find piles of these translucent wings near windowsills or light sources.

  • Termite Droppings (Frass): Drywood termites leave behind tiny, pellet-like fecal matter near their feeding areas.

Our inspectors utilize specialized tools and techniques to locate hidden infestations, including moisture meters and sounding devices. We meticulously examine basements, crawl spaces, attics, wall voids, and the perimeter of your home. Treatment Options We May Employ

  • Liquid Termite Treatments: We may use non-repellent liquid termiticides applied to the soil around your home's foundation. Termites are unable to detect these treatments and unknowingly pass through the treated zone, carrying the product back to the colony, leading to its eventual eradication.

  • Termite Baiting Systems: These systems involve strategically placing bait stations around your property containing cellulose material laced with a slow-acting termiticide. Termites feed on the bait and carry it back to the colony, sharing it with other termites and eventually leading to colony elimination. Baiting systems provide continuous monitoring and protection.

  • Direct Application to Infested Areas: In some cases, we may apply targeted treatments directly to areas of active termite infestation within your home, such as wall voids or wood members.

  • Wood Treatments: We may recommend and apply borate-based wood treatments to vulnerable wood components to make them toxic to termites.

Termites are insidious pests that can cause significant damage to your home before you even realize you have a problem. Understanding the common problems associated with termites can help you identify a potential infestation and seek professional help promptly. Ignoring the signs of termites can lead to extensive and costly repairs down the road, making early detection and intervention vital. We encounter a variety of termite-related issues in Camp Hill and the surrounding areas, each requiring a specific approach. Our expertise allows us to diagnose the problem accurately and implement the most effective solution. Here are some of the most common termite problems we address and how our services provide comprehensive solutions:

Subterranean Termite Infestations

Problem: Subterranean termites are the most common type of termite found in Pennsylvania and are responsible for the majority of termite damage. They live in underground colonies and build mud tubes to travel above ground to access wood sources. These mud tubes, often found on foundation walls, floor joists, or other structures, are a tell-tale sign of their presence. If you see these mud tubes, it's a strong indication that you have a subterranean termite infestation, and immediate action is required. These termites can quickly damage the structural integrity of your home if left unchecked, compromising the safety and value of your property. Their ability to remain hidden within the soil and wood makes them particularly challenging to detect without professional expertise. Subterranean termites are constantly foraging for new food sources, making your home a potential target if conditions are favorable. High moisture levels in basements or crawl spaces can attract subterranean termites, providing them with the necessary environment to survive and thrive. Understanding the behavior of subterranean termites is key to implementing effective control measures and preventing future infestations.

Our Solution: We utilize a combination of liquid termiticide barrier treatments and/or termite baiting systems to effectively control subterranean termites. Liquid termiticides are applied to the soil around the foundation of your home, creating a non-repellent treated zone that termites pass through, unknowingly carrying the product back to the colony. Termite baiting systems involve placing bait stations around your property containing a slow-acting termiticide that termites consume and share with other termites in the colony, leading to gradual colony elimination. Our customized treatment plan will determine the most appropriate approach based on the specific characteristics of your infestation and your property. We conduct a thorough inspection to identify all areas of termite activity and apply the treatment with precision to ensure maximum effectiveness. Our goal is to eliminate the entire colony, not just the visible termites, to provide long-lasting protection. Post-treatment monitoring is also crucial to ensure the treatment is successful and to detect any potential signs of future activity. Our expert technicians are highly trained in applying both liquid treatments and installing baiting systems, adhering to all safety guidelines and best practices. We use high-quality products that are proven to be effective in controlling subterranean termites while minimizing any potential impact on the environment or your family. Our methods are designed to be as minimally invasive as possible while still achieving complete termite eradication. We go the extra mile to ensure that the treatment is applied thoroughly and correctly, addressing all potential entry points and areas of infestation. The choice of treatment method is carefully considered based on the unique characteristics of your property and the severity of the infestation. We are committed to providing you with a comprehensive solution that will protect your home from subterranean termites for years to come.

Drywood Termite Infestations

Problem: Drywood termites are less common than subterranean termites in Pennsylvania but can still cause significant damage. Unlike subterranean termites, they do not require contact with the soil and typically infest dry, sound wood. Signs of drywood termite infestation include small piles of fecal pellets (frass), discarded wings near windows or light sources, and damaged wood
